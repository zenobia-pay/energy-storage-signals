# National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL)
# Source: https://www.nrel.gov
# Type: National Laboratory (DOE)
# Location: Golden, CO

## Overview
NREL is the U.S. Department of Energy's primary national laboratory for
renewable energy and energy efficiency research and development.

## Key Energy Storage Research Areas

### Grid Integration Studies
- Storage dispatch modeling
- Grid flexibility studies
- High penetration renewable scenarios
- Capacity credit analysis

### Technology Assessment
- Battery technology evaluation
- Long-duration storage analysis
- Hydrogen storage
- Thermal energy storage

### Tools and Models
- ReEDS (Regional Energy Deployment System)
- SAM (System Advisor Model)
- REopt (renewable energy optimization)
- Cambium (grid scenario data)

### Standard Scenarios
Annual long-term grid scenarios incorporating:
- Solar, wind, storage deployment
- Cost projections
- Policy impacts
- Regional variations

## Notable Energy Storage Research

### Storage Futures Study
Comprehensive analysis of grid-scale energy storage needs:
- 2020 baseline to 2050 projections
- Cost-optimal storage deployment
- Technology mix requirements
- Regional storage needs

### Utility-Scale Battery Storage
- Cost trends and projections
- Performance characteristics
- Degradation modeling
- Use case economics

### Long-Duration Storage
- Multi-day storage requirements
- Technology comparison
- Cost targets for competitiveness
- Grid reliability contributions
